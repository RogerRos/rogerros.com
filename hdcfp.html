Biography
Who I Am
Major & Career ▶

As this web page might suggest, my major is in computer science. I chose CS because I am an ambitious person, and to build great things, tools are an indisputable requirement. Unlike manual tools, digital ones are automatizations which, along with the clean practices and the logical and meticulous structure that make up programming, I found it was the most suitable area for my interests.

Future Life ▶

My interest in both economy and software converged in cryptocurrencies. I am also a user of GNU/Linux, specifically Arch Linux, which, like all GNU/Linux distributions, is open source. Similarly, to ensure the efficiency of the blockchain, its code must be open source too, as the main idea behind it is that the ledger is public and copied across multiple nodes to ensure the immutability of transactions. So, cryptocurrencies are my main field right now, and I am working on an algorithm.

Philosophy is also a fundamental part of my way of thinking. Considering the idea that a person could be synthesized into a set of ideas, and that the operation over those ideas to make actions would be equivalent to the person making those actions, the way to ensure one's survival is overcoming the physical limitations to capture one's ideas in an immutable structure. In other words, I will create a company that follows my ideas.

Something Fun ▶

The funniest thing about me is also my biggest irony. I am torn between two action frameworks: perfectionism and pragmatism. Perfectionism drives me toward order, cleanliness, optimization and efficiency. Pragmatism pulls me toward greed, explosive growth and self-interest. And the ironic connection with my personal life is that I seek connection with others, but my rational nature is precisely what stops me from achieving it.

Bach and classical in general for my perfectionist side, heavy metal for my pragmatic times, and pop music when I think I have made an emotional connection. Spoiler: it always ends in heavy metal a few days later.

Key Resources ▶

The 3 resources that helped me were: IP Office, Information Office and the university gym. With my classmate Dale we made a presentation about mental wellness, you can view the presentation by pressing the button below.

Best Advice ▶

My best advice to my first-day-of-school self to improve in college would be to keep a periodic review of assignments to not miss any and be able to plan ahead. But where I would probably give myself the most advice would be in time management, and I would probably tell myself that the personal projects are going well and that there is no reason to wake up at 3 in the morning to check if the hyperparameter optimizer is converging.

Unexpected Insight ▶

That teachers are sometimes willing to be more flexible, accept some assignments a bit later than the agreed date, and in general it is not as rigid as I thought, even though the preferred option is always to be punctual with deadlines.

Reflection
Looking Back
01 / 07
The most helpful thing from this course that shifted the way I will approach my future learning journey is something surprisingly simple: maintaining a periodic review of assignments to not miss any and be able to plan ahead.
02 / 07
It sounds obvious, but having a system to track deadlines prevents me from either overplanning everything to an obsessive degree or leaving it all to the last moment in a burst of pragmatic efficiency.
03 / 07
But where the course really changed my approach was in time management. I have a tendency to let personal projects consume me, and I would probably tell my first-day-of-class self that the projects are going well and that there is no reason to wake up at 3 in the morning to check if the hyperparameter optimizer is converging.
04 / 07
The course also taught me that teachers are sometimes willing to be more flexible, accept some assignments a bit later than the agreed date, and in general it is not as rigid as I thought, even though the preferred option is always to be punctual with deadlines.
05 / 07
I have met people and I have discovered that I am more sociable than I thought. I hope some of them will maintain contact with me.
06 / 07
I was reviewing the bingo game from the first day and I saw the names and I felt that, even weak due to the short time we have spent together, there was an emotional bond with the names.
07 / 07
If there is something I truly learned from myself is that I am not as rational as I thought, which is great, but now I have a new world of possibilities to discover.
